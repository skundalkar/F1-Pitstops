# Product Roadmap

## Milestone 1: Pre-race strategy brief

**Goal:** in under two minutes, an enthusiast understands the strategic shape
of an upcoming race and can compare a few sensible plans.

### 1. Race briefing

Show circuit, race distance, pit-lane time loss, available compounds, forecast,
overtaking difficulty, and strategic watch-outs.

**Done when:**

- One view explains the likely stop-count range and why.
- Every factual input shows its source and update time.
- Weather and Safety Car assumptions are visibly uncertain.
- A user can tell whether track position or fresh tyres is likely to matter more.

### 2. Driver lineup and grid context

Let users choose up to five drivers and understand their grid position,
starting tyre when known, comparable race-pace indicator, and strategic
opportunity or risk.

**Done when:**

- A lineup can be saved and reloaded locally.
- Every driver has a likely plan, alternative plan, biggest risk, and strategic
  upside.
- Missing qualifying, tyre, or weather data is explicit rather than inferred.
- Traffic and undercut exposure are understandable from the grid.

### 3. Three-plan scenario comparison

Compare conservative, balanced, and aggressive tyre/pit plans with editable,
transparent assumptions.

**Done when:**

- Users can alter Safety Car chance, tyre degradation, and rain assumptions.
- Each plan shows stint tyres, pit windows, trade-offs, and an invalidating
  condition.
- A "why this changed" explanation updates with scenario inputs.
- The tool makes no finishing-position accuracy claim at this stage.

## Explicitly deferred

- Bulk ingestion of every historical race.
- Automated strategy optimization and machine learning.
- Live timing and automated in-race recommendations.
- Telemetry-heavy views and video rendering.
- Multi-user editing and fantasy-platform integrations.

These are later phases, after the first brief is understandable and trustworthy.
